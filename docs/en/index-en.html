– Recommended Places –

Nara Park

Kasuga Taisha Shrine

Kasuga Taisha, with a history of 1,300 years, was founded in 768 to protect the Heijo-kyo Capital and to pray for the prosperity of the people.

Kofukuji Temple

The temple houses a statue of Ashura, designated as a national treasure, and many other temple treasures.

Ukigumoenchi

It is also the largest lawn in Nara Park and one of the best places to relax in Nara Park with its spacious lawn. It is also a good place to take a short break.

Nara National Museum

The second oldest national museum in Japan after the Tokyo National Museum, it has 13 exhibition halls.

Mt. Wakakusa

The most famous scenic spot in Nara City is a small mountain 342 meters above sea level. It commands a panoramic view of Nara City and is said to have the best view in Nara.

Shosoin

A large stilt warehouse built in the Koukura style where treasures including the relics of Emperor Shomu, the founder of Todaiji Temple, are stored.

Arashiyama

Togetsukyo Bridge

The Togetsukyo Bridge over the Katsura River is the symbol of Arashiyama.

Bamboo Groves

The Bamboo Groves connecting Nomiya Shrine to Okochi Villa is a fantastic bamboo grove that stretches for about 400 meters.

Tenryuji Temple

Tenryuji Temple was founded in 1339 by Ashikaga Takauji.

Sagano Scenic Railway

This sightseeing railroad allows visitors to enjoy the rich natural scenery of Sagano from the windows of the scenic railway that runs over the cliff.

Hozu River Boat Tour

This activity is a boat ride down the Hozu River, which flows from Kameoka to Saga Arashiyama.

Kimono Forest

The sight of approximately 600 poles lined up with glossy yuzen fabrics is beautiful and full of Kyoto's culture atmosphere.

Ikoma City

Ikoma Mountain Amusement Park

A family amusement park located on the summit of the mountain, this amusement has a long history park was opened in 1929.

Kuragaritoge

NomiyaThe Kuragaritoge, a Nara Prefecture scenic asset, has long been known as a roadway from Osaka to Nara and Ise.

Ikoma submontane Parks

The park also offers campgrounds and a wide variety of facilities and programs.

hygge

You can enjoy homemade muffins and specialty drinks.

Shigi Ikoma Skyline

The road is famous for its night views of the Osaka area and Ikoma city.

Kyoto Station

Kyoto Station Building

One of the largest station building facilities in Japan. It is a large-scale complex facility with a hotel, department stores, cultural facilities, etc.

Kyoto Tower

A landmark of Kyoto that is the first thing you see when you get off at Kyoto Station.

Kyoto Aquarium

Japan's first large-scale inland aquarium.

East Hongwanji Temple

Many national treasures. It is also registered as a World Heritage site.

Toji Temple

Toji Temple, the head temple of the Shingon sect of Buddhism. It is the only existing remains of the Heian-kyo capital and is one of the World Heritage sites.

Tofukuji Temple

There are approximately 2,000 maple trees on the grounds.

Kiyomizu Temple

The temple is known for its "Kiyomizu-no-butai".

Shaved ice stores near Kyoto Station

Saryo Tsujiri
Kyoto Isetan store

Saryo Tsujiri Kyoto Isetan Store, located near Kyoto Station, is a cafe where you can enjoy shaved ice, parfaits, and other green tea menu items using our special Uji green tea.

Nakamura Tokichi Honten
Kyoto Station Store

A store where you can enjoy a menu of sweets using tea in a stylish Japanese modern space.

Tully's Coffee
Kyoto Avanti Store

A casual café recommended for mornings and breaks.

Ohara, Kurama, Kibune

Kibune Shrine

Kifune Kifune Shrine is dedicated to the god of water, Takanagokami, who is mentioned in the Kojiki and Nihonshoki.

Ohari Box

A store and workshop dealing in hair ornaments and Kyoto accessories. Traditional Tsumazaiku hair ornaments are special items that add a touch of glamour to special occasions such as weddings and graduation ceremonies.

Kamigamo Shrine

Kamigamo Shrine, located in the northern part of Kyoto City, is known as the oldest shrine in Kyoto.

Rurikoin Temple

Rurikoin Temple, located on the banks of the clear Takano River, has become a popular spot for its beautiful Toko-momiji reflections on the polished floor.

Enryakuji Temple on Mt. Hieizan

Enryakuji Temple on Mt. Hieizan is an ancient temple with a vast area of Keidai-itchi that stretches from Kyoto City to Otsu City in Shiga Prefecture.

– Recommended Shops –

Japanese confectionery

Unbaked Yatsuhashi "Yuko"

The unbaked Yatsuhashi of Izutsu's tradition and the moist sweet bean paste go well together, a confectionery associated with Kyoto.

Demachiyanagi "Meiyo Mamemochi"

The "Mamemochi", commonly known as "Mamemochi", is a homemade sweet bean paste wrapped in a soft but firm rice cake. The combination of the soft but firm rice cake, smooth red bean paste, and moderately salty red peas is exquisite.

Tsuruya Yoshinobu "Yumohci"

One bite and the chewy texture of the gyuhi dough, the refined sweetness of the fine wasanbon, and the aroma of yuzu blend together in the mouth.

Ogura Sanso "Arare Dutsumi"

Its sweet and spicy flavor, sesame aroma, and light texture are widely favored.

Shiohoken Shioken "Yuki Maroge"

When you put it in your mouth, it crumbles and melts away like a snow.

Kyuemon Ito "Uji Matcha Dakufu"

The rich green tea bean paste, a combination of fresh cream and refined sweetness of wasanbon, is a perfect match with the fluffy, doughy dough.

Western confectionery

Oanocha Langdosha "Cha no Kwa"

The strong aroma and taste of dark tea is as strong as the baked dough. And the milk-filled white chocolate sandwiched between them is a perfect match.

Crochet "Kyo Ame" series

In addition to "green tea azuki bean flavor" and "yuzu flavor," there are also unexpected Western flavors such as "shortcake flavor" and "peach yogurt flavor," making for a total of more than 80 varieties.

Garam Masala "Masarapi"

Selected peanuts are coated several times with an original spice blend dough. The spiciness of the spice is balanced by the moderate sweetness of the peanuts, creating an irresistible aftertaste.

Kyoto Kameyoshi "Popcorn"

Its sweet and spicy flavor, sesame aroma, and light texture are widely favored.

Gion Tsujiri "Tsujiri no Sato"

The rich and fragrant green tea cream and the crunchy texture of the carefully baked rolled rice crackers are addictive.
